📖 How auditing works in Forge

1. You verify

Read the AC + assigner notes. Run the prescribed verification (script, manual walkthrough, log inspection). Form your verdict.

2. Forge records

Submit a verdict + evidence. Forge captures your name, timestamp, evidence hash, and the exact text you wrote. This becomes part of the permanent audit trail.

3. Evidence is required for PASSED_WITH_EVIDENCE

You must upload at least one artifact (screenshot, .log, junit.xml) or paste evidence text ≥200 chars. Without evidence, the option is PASSED_ATTESTATION (sign-off only) — visually distinct in the audit trail.

PASSED_WITH_EVIDENCE — verdict + uploaded artifact / pasted text. Strongest record. Recommended for HIPAA / GDPR / SOC2 AC.
PASSED_ATTESTATION — your sign-off only, no captured artifact. Allowed but tagged "auditor sign-off, no artifact" in audit log. Use only when evidence is genuinely impossible (e.g., live demo with client present).
REJECTED — task auto-re-opens; finding F-NN created with your reason text. Assigner must address before re-submitting.
NEEDS_CLARIFICATION — back-and-forth with assigner. Decision Q-NNN auto-spawned. AC stays AWAITING_AUDITOR.
🔎 What you CANNOT do here
  • Edit AC text — if the AC is wrong / unverifiable / ambiguous, use NEEDS_CLARIFICATION. The assigner edits, not you.
  • Close the task — only the project owner closes tasks. Your verdict feeds in but doesn't close anything by itself.
  • Reassign to someone else — if you can't audit (vacation, conflict of interest), request reassignment via NEEDS_CLARIFICATION; the assigner re-routes.
  • Comment on unrelated tasks — your access is scoped to AC's explicitly assigned to you. Other tasks on this project are invisible.
  • See production data directly — Forge does not grant you DB access. You audit against artifacts the assigner provides.
  • Override your own past verdicts — once submitted, a verdict is permanent (unless REJECTED → re-opened by assigner).
